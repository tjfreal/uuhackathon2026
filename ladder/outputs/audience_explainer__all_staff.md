# Note System for Library Colleagues (All-Staff)

A personal knowledge system that lives on your own machine. You capture notes in plain text, and a set of tools helps you revisit old ideas, notice connections, and explain the system clearly to other people.

The library-as-a-ladder idea started as a people-first approach to help-desk and library service: meet people where they are and help them reach the next rung. This project carries that approach into note-system scaffolds and knowledge work in the automation age.

Ladder is part of the system because explanation is part of the work. If the system cannot explain itself to different kinds of people, it has not finished the job.

## Why it matters for this audience

personal and practical — here is what I built and why it might matter in a library context.

The goal here is not to hand you a full technical architecture. It is to give you a reachable next rung into the system from where you actually are starting.

## The system in brief

- Notes get captured and forgotten. Search helps you find things you already know to look for. This system is aimed at helping you find things you forgot you knew and making that process teachable to other people.
- Everything runs on your machine. No subscription. No cloud account required. Your files stay yours.
- The work spans foundation, retrieval, organization, sensemaking, communication, and presentation.

## A few parts worth noticing

### Note System

It is a way to keep useful knowledge from disappearing into old notes and forgotten documents.

the user keeps daily notes in plain text, then uses review passes and local tooling to revisit ideas that would otherwise sink back into the archive.

Status: built and in active use.

### Surface

It helps useful things you already wrote come back when they matter again instead of staying buried in old notes.

During a weekly review, Surface could show the user a note from two years ago about library service that suddenly matters again while he is designing Ladder.

Status: proposal.

### Ladder

It gives you a readable way into the system without assuming you already speak its technical language.

The same Surface component can be explained to library colleagues as a way of bringing back useful old notes, and to collaborators as a retrieval and explainability problem.

Status: in progress.

## Honest limits

- Review still depends on consistent human habits
- It is still a proposal rather than a finished tool
- It is in progress and only covers a first slice of the ecosystem so far
